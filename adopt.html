Thanks For Thinking About Adopting A Dog!

Here's What You Can Expect From The Process

We love seeing our dogs matched with the perfect home. At Second Chance Dog Rescue, we make it our mission to set our pups up in the right environment for their needs. Therefore, each application is given our full attention and consideration, and follows a specific set of steps. Read below to learn more about our process



GENERAL ADOPTION PROCESS

SCDR's regular adoption application process involves the following steps:

1.  Getting to know you. Fill out the adoption application and pre-interview questionnaire. We will also check vet (if applicable) and personal references, and will ask for virtual home visits or photos.

2.  Interview. If we believe the adoption is a good match, a member of our adoption team will reach out to you for an interview.

3.  Meet & Greet. You and all members of your home will have a chance to meet the dog (potentially virtually). This is a great opportunity to ask the foster any questions.

4.  The official stuff. You will be sent the adoption contract to review and sign. This is also when you pay the adoption fee.

5.  Adoption Trial. Each adoption will have a trial period before the adoption is finalized. See how your pup settles into your home and routine.

6.  The good part. After the adoption trial, you will receive your adoption certificate.

If at any point in the process it is determined this dog is not the best fit, dedicated applicants can choose to keep their applications on file for another dog, or even join our adoption waitlist. This process will be explained at the time of processing.



ADOPTION FEES

•  Puppies up to 1 year $890

•  Dogs 1 year and older $720

If a dog is adopted under the age of six months and is not altered, an additional $100 deposit is required. Please note this deposit is refundable upon completion of spay/neuter (proof of surgery is required). More explained during the adoption process

(Effective January 1st, 2022) Due to the increasing hardships to access in need dogs, Second Chance's current cost per dog is over $1000. Therefore, we have had to raise our fees to accomodate this change



ADOPTION FAQS

Where are you located?

SCDR does not have a facility. We prefer to see dogs in foster homes to adjust to their new surroundings as we learn what a suitable furever home looks like. Our dogs are fostered throughout cities in Southwestern Ontario.

Do you adopt dogs out all over Canada?

SCDR adopts dogs out mostly in Southwestern Ontario. If you're looking for dogs in other provinces, please contact your local animal shelter/rescues. If you're in Ontario, you must be willing to drive to pick-up the dog where they are currently fostered. You may also be required to travel to meet and greets, and initial vet appointments.

How long does the adoption process take?

The adoption process can take anywhere from a few days to a few weeks. It depends on availability of the applicants and our volunteers. Please only apply if you are prepared to welcome the dog immediately. We do not hold dogs for any reason

I've submitted an application but haven't heard back yet - what should I do?

Please give our adoption coordinators up to 5 days to respond to your submitted application, we do respond to everyone who applies. Please also check your junk folder as sometimes our emails end up there. If you don't receive an email from us after 5 days and have checked your junk folder, you can send a follow up email to adoption@secondchancedogrescue.ca We appreciate your patience!

Are all your dogs potty trained?

Our awesome foster parents work with their foster dogs on all basic training, including potty training. However, you should expect for your dog to potentially regress once you take them home to a new environment. For adoption trials where the applicant is welcoming and out-of-country intake straight into their home, all basic training including potty training will be required. That said, this is one of the least common areas of support fosters & adopters need in our care. You're got this!

Will my application be rejected if I don't have a backyard?

Definitely not! It's important to read the bio of the specific dog you're applying for in case there is a requirement for a fenced yard. However, we have many dogs who are suited to condo/apartment living, and can thrive in this environment once they get enough exercise and mental stimulation
